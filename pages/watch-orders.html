#01
SHONEN

NARUTO (SKIP THE FILLER)

Naruto's 720 episodes contain roughly 40% filler. Here's exactly how to watch it without wasting time.

📋 RECOMMENDED ORDER:

  1. Naruto — Eps 1-135 (Land of Waves -> Sasuke Recovery)
  2. Skip to Shippuden — Eps 136-220 are ALL filler
  3. Shippuden — Eps 1-56 (Kazekage Rescue + Tenchi Bridge)
  4. Shippuden — Eps 72-126 (Akatsuki Suppression + Itachi)
  5. Shippuden — Eps 152-175 (Pain's Assault — Peak Fiction)
  6. Shippuden — Eps 197-222 (Five Kage Summit)
  7. Shippuden — Eps 261-321, 339-366, 372-393, 414-426, 459-479, 484-500 (War Arc -> Finale)
#02
FANTASY

FATE SERIES

Notoriously confusing. Multiple timelines, studios, and storylines. Here is the Ufotable Route.

🅰️ THE CORE STORY (UFOTABLE ROUTE):

  1. Fate/Zero (2011) — The prequel. Dark, mature, and the best starting point.
  2. Fate/Stay Night: Unlimited Blade Works (2014) — The main story (Rin's route).
  3. Fate/Stay Night: Heaven's Feel (2017-2020) — 3 film trilogy. The darkest and final core route.

🅱️ FATE/GRAND ORDER (MOBILE GAME TIMELINE):

  1. First Order (2016) — Prologue movie.
  2. Camelot (2020-2021) — 2 film series (Singularity 6).
  3. Absolute Demonic Front: Babylonia (2019) — TV Series (Singularity 7).
  4. Solomon (2021) — The Grand Temple of Time movie.

🔀 ALTERNATE UNIVERSES & SPIN-OFFS (WATCH IN ANY ORDER):

  • Fate/Apocrypha — A massive 14 vs 14 servant holy grail war in a parallel universe.
  • Fate/Extra Last Encore — Cyberpunk Fate set on the moon. Very confusing.
  • Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya — Magical girl spin-off starring Illya.
  • Lord El-Melloi II's Case Files — A mystery series following Waver Velvet after Fate/Zero.
  • Today's Menu for the Emiya Family — Pure wholesome cooking slice-of-life.
  • Fate/strange Fake — A chaotic, rule-breaking Grail War in America.

#04
MECHA / PSYCHOLOGICAL

NEON GENESIS EVANGELION

The original 90s masterpiece vs the modern Rebuild films. You must watch both.

📺 PHASE 1: THE ORIGINAL SERIES:

  1. Neon Genesis Evangelion (1995) — Episodes 1 to 24.
  2. Neon Genesis Evangelion (1995) — Episodes 25 & 26 (The psychological TV ending).
  3. The End of Evangelion (1997) — The feature film that replaces/runs parallel to episodes 25 & 26. This is the "true" cinematic ending.

🎥 PHASE 2: THE REBUILD FILMS (Alternate Retelling/Sequel):

  1. Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone — A modern remake of eps 1-6.
  2. Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance — The story completely diverges here.
  3. Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo — A completely new, wild direction.
  4. Evangelion: 3.0+1.0 Thrice Upon a Time — The final, definitive conclusion to the entire franchise.
#05
ACTION / BIZARRE

JOJO'S BIZARRE ADVENTURE

Each 'Part' focuses on a different generation of the Joestar bloodline. DO NOT SKIP PARTS.

📺 THE ANIME TIMELINE (PARTS 1-6):

  1. JoJo's Bizarre Adventure (2012) — Contains Part 1 (Phantom Blood) and Part 2 (Battle Tendency).
  2. Stardust Crusaders (2014) & Battle in Egypt (2015) — Part 3. The introduction of Stands.
  3. Diamond is Unbreakable (2016) — Part 4. A small-town murder mystery.
  4. Thus Spoke Kishibe Rohan (OVA) — Spin-off featuring Rohan. Watch after Part 4.
  5. Golden Wind (2018) — Part 5. Mafia action in Italy.
  6. Stone Ocean (2021) — Part 6. The grand finale of the original timeline.

📖 THE MANGA TIMELINE (THE SBR UNIVERSE):

  • Part 7: Steel Ball Run — A completely new universe. A cross-country horse race in 1890 America.
  • Part 8: JoJolion — A mystery set in an alternate Morioh town.
  • Part 9: The JOJOLands — The current ongoing manga series.
#06
SCI-FI / MAGIC

A CERTAIN SCIENTIFIC / MAGICAL SERIES (TOARU)

A massive shared universe between magic users and scientific espers. The spin-off (Railgun) is often considered better than the main series (Index).

📋 SIMPLIFIED / RECOMMENDED WATCH ORDER:

  1. A Certain Scientific Railgun (Season 1) — The best introduction to Academy City.
  2. A Certain Scientific Railgun S (Season 2) — Covers the peak arc of the franchise.
  3. A Certain Magical Index (Season 1) — The main storyline begins.
  4. A Certain Scientific Accelerator (Spin-off) — Best watched after Index 1.
  5. A Certain Magical Index II (Season 2)
  6. A Certain Scientific Railgun T (Season 3)
  7. A Certain Magical Index: The Miracle of Endymion (Movie)
  8. A Certain Magical Index III (Season 3)
#07
MECHA / MILITARY

MOBILE SUIT GUNDAM (UNIVERSAL CENTURY)

Gundam has many stand-alone universes (like Iron-Blooded Orphans or 00). But the main, original timeline is the 'Universal Century' (UC). Here is the core UC path.

📋 THE CORE UC TIMELINE:

  1. Mobile Suit Gundam (0079) — The original 1979 series (or the 3 compilation movies).
  2. Mobile Suit Zeta Gundam — The legendary sequel. (Do NOT watch the compilation movies for this, watch the TV series).
  3. Mobile Suit Gundam ZZ — The direct follow-up to Zeta.
  4. Mobile Suit Gundam: Char's Counterattack — The 1988 film. The epic conclusion to the Amuro vs Char rivalry.

⭐ ESSENTIAL SIDE STORIES (Watch after the core):

  • Gundam 0080: War in the Pocket — A tragic, intimate look at the One Year War.
  • The 08th MS Team — Gritty, ground-level jungle warfare.
  • Gundam Thunderbolt — High-intensity jazz and brutal space combat.
  • Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn — The lavishly produced epilogue to the early UC era.
  • Mobile Suit Gundam: Hathaway — The next era of the UC timeline.

#08
SCI-FI / THRILLER

SCIENCE ADVENTURE (STEINS;GATE)

The Steins;Gate watch order is heavily debated due to time travel mechanics. This is the definitive chronological/emotional order.

⏳ THE TRUE ENDING ORDER:

  1. Steins;Gate (Episodes 1 to 22).
  2. Steins;Gate Episode 23 (Beta) / Open the Missing Link — The alternate version of episode 23 where Okabe gives up.
  3. Steins;Gate 0 — The entire sequel series exploring the timeline where Okabe gave up.
  4. Steins;Gate (Episodes 23 & 24) — Return to the original series for the true, triumphant ending.

🔬 THE WIDER UNIVERSE:

Steins;Gate is part of the 'Science Adventure' visual novel universe. If you are interested in the others, watch them in this order, but note their anime adaptations are considered inferior to Steins;Gate.

  • Chaos;Head — Watch first.
  • Robotics;Notes — Watch after Steins;Gate.
  • Chaos;Child — Watch after Chaos;Head.
#09
DARK FANTASY

BERSERK

The anime adaptations of Berserk are notoriously fragmented. Here is the best way to experience Guts' journey.

⚔️ THE RECOMMENDED PATH:

  1. Berserk (1997) — The classic 25-episode anime. Covers the Golden Age arc beautifully.
  2. Berserk: The Golden Age Arc (Memorial Edition) — A remastered movie trilogy. You can watch this instead of the 1997 anime if you prefer modern animation, though it cuts some character development.
  3. Read the Manga — From chapter 1. The 2016/2017 CGI anime adaptations are universally considered terrible and skip major arcs. The only true way to experience the rest of Berserk is the manga.

SHONEN

BLEACH

Bleach is 45% filler. If you don't skip the filler, the pacing will ruin the show for you.

🚫 THE FILLER-FREE PATH:

SKIP the following episodes completely: 33, 50, 64-108 (The Bount Arc), 128-137, 147-149, 168-189, 204-205, 213-214, 227-265, 287, 298-299, 303-305, 311-341, 355.

  1. Bleach (Original Series) — Watch the canon episodes only.
  2. Bleach: Thousand-Year Blood War (TYBW) — The incredible modern revival that adapts the final arc of the manga.
#13
SHONEN / ACTION

DRAGON BALL

The grandfather of shonen. With GT, Super, Kai, and movies, it can get confusing.

🐉 THE CANON TIMELINE:

  1. Dragon Ball — The original adventure with kid Goku.
  2. Dragon Ball Z (or Dragon Ball Kai) — Kai is a remastered version of Z with the filler cut out. Highly recommended.
  3. Dragon Ball Super — The official continuation. (You can watch the movies Battle of Gods and Resurrection 'F' instead of the first 27 episodes of Super).
  4. Dragon Ball Super: Broly (Movie)
  5. Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero (Movie)
  6. Dragon Ball Daima — The newest canon series.

Note: Dragon Ball GT is not canon to the main manga timeline, but is a fun alternate universe watch.

#14
ADVENTURE

ONE PIECE

1000+ episodes is daunting. Here is the optimal way to consume the greatest adventure ever told.

🏴‍☠️ THE GRAND LINE PATH:

  • Option 1: One Pace — A fan project that meticulously edits the anime to match the manga's pacing, cutting out hundreds of hours of dragged-out scenes. Highly recommended for the post-timeskip arcs.
  • Option 2: The Manga — The absolute best pacing and art. Read the colored version if you prefer.
  • Option 3: The Remake (The One Piece) — Wait for the upcoming WIT Studio remake which will condense the early arcs.

Essential Movies (Watch these when you catch up):

  • One Piece Film: Strong World
  • One Piece Film: Z
  • One Piece Film: Gold
  • One Piece: Stampede
  • One Piece Film: Red

DEMON SLAYER (KIMETSU NO YAIBA)

The TV series and Movies overlap, causing immense confusion for newcomers.

🌊 THE BREATHING ORDER:

  1. Season 1 (26 episodes)
  2. Mugen Train Arc — You have a choice: watch the Mugen Train Movie OR the Mugen Train TV Arc (7 episodes). They are the exact same story. The TV arc just has one extra original episode at the start.
  3. Entertainment District Arc (Season 2)
  4. Swordsmith Village Arc (Season 3)
  5. Hashira Training Arc (Season 4)
  6. Infinity Castle Arc — Upcoming movie trilogy finale.
#16
MYSTERY / ACTION

BACCANO!

Baccano! is famously told out of chronological order, jumping between the 1930s, 1931, and 1932 simultaneously.

🚂 HOW TO WATCH IT:

  • The Broadcast Order (Eps 1-13 + 3 OVAs) — Do NOT try to watch this chronologically. The entire genius of the show is how the disparate timelines crash into each other. Watch it exactly as it was released.
  • Tip: The first episode is incredibly confusing. Stick with it until episode 4, and the pieces of the puzzle will start violently clicking together.
#17
THRILLER / MYSTERY

DANGANRONPA

Danganronpa 3 is an anime-original conclusion to the games, and it has the most insane airing order in anime history.

🐻 THE ALTERNATING ORDER:

You must play Danganronpa 1, 2, and Ultra Despair Girls before watching this. Danganronpa 3: The End of Hope's Peak High School was broadcast as two separate shows simultaneously. You MUST alternate episodes.

  1. Future Arc Episode 1
  2. Despair Arc Episode 1
  3. Future Arc Episode 2
  4. Despair Arc Episode 2
  5. ...Continue alternating until Future Arc 12...
  6. Despair Arc Episode 11
  7. Hope Arc (The 1-episode grand finale)
#18
SLICE OF LIFE / SCI-FI

THE MELANCHOLY OF HARUHI SUZUMIYA

Famously broadcast out of chronological order in 2006 to preserve the climax, then rebroadcast in chronological order with new episodes in 2009.

👽 WHICH ORDER TO CHOOSE?:

  • Chronological Order (2009 Broadcast) — This is the recommended order for modern viewers. It places the 2006 and 2009 episodes in the correct timeline, leading perfectly into the movie.
  • The Endless Eight Warning: Episodes 12 through 19 of the 2009 order are the exact same time loop animated slightly differently 8 times. Most people watch episodes 1, 2, and 8 of the loop and skip the rest.
  • The Disappearance of Haruhi Suzumiya (Movie) — The absolute masterpiece conclusion. Watch immediately after the TV series.
